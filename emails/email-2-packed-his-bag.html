The gym bag story I mentioned.

A client of mine packed his gym bag every single night for three weeks. Put it by the front door. Set his alarm for 6am. Did everything right.

He never went. Not once.

Every morning he'd look at the bag, feel something between guilt and dread, and leave it there. By week three he messaged me and said he wanted to cancel.

Most coaches would've given him a speech about discipline. Told him to push through. I asked him one question: "What happens between seeing the bag and deciding not to go?"

Long pause. Then: "I think about how long it's been since I went. And the longer it's been, the worse it feels to go back. So I don't."

That's the bit nobody talks about. The gap doesn't close with time. It widens. Every day you don't go makes the next day harder. Not because you're lazy. Because your brain is doing exactly what brains evolved to do: protect you from situations where you feel out of your depth.

Here's something you can use right now. Next time you skip a session, don't ask yourself "why am I so lazy?" Ask: "what made not going easier than going?" The answer is usually something boring and fixable. The gym is too far. The session is too long. You don't know what to do when you get there.

He did eventually go. But what happened when he got there surprised both of us. He said something I'd never heard a client say before.

I'll tell you what it was next time.
